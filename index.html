Leverage and meeting goals

Key client contact motivation

Carrots

Relevant benefits that your products and services may help a Client achieve - these give you leverage and motivate Client action. Consider all potential types of benefits - technical, operational, financial and personal.

:

Sticks

Relevant Client concerns, risks or fears that your products and services might help a Client overcome or avoid. These may be technical, operational, financial or personal. Remember that avoiding a loss is often a more powerful client motivator than gaining a benefit.

Meeting Goals

Realistic Advance

:

Optimistic Advance

Game plan

Opening Statement

Plan the Opening - a confident, concise first minute will dramatically increase the meeting's productivity. Use bullet points, not a script. Your opening may include the meeting purpose, a proposed agenda (including gaining permission to drive the meeting), any datapoints that give you leverage, and confirmation of time available.

:

Steps required in this Meeting

After your opening statement, which steps will you require in this meeting? DISCOVER (exchanging info), PROPOSE (proposing an advance/next step), CLOSE (overcoming objections to gain agreement to the advance). Remember that you may not need every step in every meeting. e.g., if you have already exchanged all key information prior to a meeting, you may choose to skip the DISCOVER step and jump straight to PROPOSE.

:

Ask (Top 3 questions)

Prioritize and distill the three most important questions to ask in this meeting. Focus on areas of leverage, where you can create and monetize value for the Client.

:

Tell (Top 3 data points to share)

Prioritize and distill no more than 3 concise items of information or data to share with the Client in this meeting. Focus on information that gives you credibility /leverage and/or that manages Client expectations.

:

Draft proposal

Based on the meeting goals, what proposal would you like to be able to make in this meeting to secure the Advance you want? If you plan a proposal in advance, you are more likely to make it, and hence more likely to advance the ball.

:

Top 3 Objections

Prioritize and distill the 3 worst objections that you may get to your advance, and consider the best way to overcome these objections. Where possible, pre-empt likely objections before they are raised by the Client.
